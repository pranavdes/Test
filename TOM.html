AV QAR Operating Model Framework

1. Introduction

Purpose

Document the Operating Model Framework for the Powai Center of Excellence for the AV QAR process.

Objectives

  • Achieve the firm-wide goal of standardisation and globalisation of the AV QAR process within the Control & Oversight team.
  • Optimise resource efficiencies through streamlined resource utilisation.
  • Enhance operational resilience.
  • Consolidate best practices across regions.

Context & Background

AV QAR (Account Verification Quality Assurance Review) is a process run across all regions as a control to further strengthen the Global AV Policy. Currently, the process is executed in a siloed manner with varying process implementations and policy interpretations across regions. This results in:

  • Confusion among controllers who must prepare different substantiations and supporting documents for similar account types.
  • Inconsistencies in account gradings, leading to queries regarding cross-region differences.

The directive from FOC to standardise and globalise processes—and to utilise Powai as a hub—has guided the development of the proposed Powai Center of Excellence Hub and Spoke Model.

4. Roles & Responsibilities

  • Powai C&O Head:
    • Member of the Powai Leadership Team.
    • Point of Contact for Senior Management Steer.
  • Global C&O Powai Lead:
    • Ensure the smooth running of the AV QAR process.
    • Manage service delivery in line with established SLAs.
    • Oversee capacity planning and overall process management.
  • AV QAR Co-Leads:
    • Handle day-to-day operations of the AV QAR process.
    • Train team members.
    • Interface with account owners to discuss findings.
    • Publish MIS and interface with regional leads.
    • Present findings to senior management.
  • AV QAR Team Members:
    • Execute testing and review activities as required.
    • Interface with controllers to resolve queries.
    • Perform additional tasks as directed by the AV QAR Co-Leads.

6. Selection Process

Criteria

Candidates should be:

  • Competent accountants with a strong foundation in accounting principles.
  • Experienced in account verification quality assurance review or a similar process.
  • Possess relevant years of work experience as per the hiring band requirements.

Assessment & Onboarding

A minimum of two rounds of elimination-style interviews will be conducted. The interview process will include questions on:

  • Accounting principles.
  • Challenging QAR reviews.
  • Operational risk and investment banking fundamentals.

Continuous Talent Review

AV QAR Co-Leads will monitor team progress, conduct periodic performance reviews, and recommend skill development initiatives.

7. Turnaround Time (TAT) & Performance Metrics

Turnaround Time (TAT): To be aligned with the TAT defined in the AV QAR Procedure Document.

Performance Metrics & KPIs: AV QAR Leads will collaborate with regional heads to develop or leverage region-specific KPIs. Monthly performance overviews should include, but not be limited to:

  • Completeness
  • Accuracy
  • Timeliness
  • Regional Coverage (or any additional relevant aspect)

8. Process Flow & Workflow

The AV QAR process flow and detailed workflow should be implemented in accordance with the AV QAR Procedure Document. All process steps, decision points, and escalation paths must be clearly documented and accessible.

9. Governance & Reporting Structure

Oversight Mechanism

  • AV QAR Co-Leads: Provide weekly updates to regional heads/delegates via email, Webex, Teams, or other appropriate mediums.
  • Global C&O Lead: Discuss overall process performance with regional leads on a monthly basis.
  • Regional Heads: May escalate issues to the Powai C&O Head as needed.

Reporting Protocol

  • AV QAR MIS: Published as per the frequency defined in the AV QAR Procedure Document.
  • Performance KPIs: Reported on a monthly basis.

Escalation Procedure

Follow the escalation procedures outlined in the AV QAR Procedure Document.

12. Continuous Improvement & Change Management

Feedback Loop

The team will document and track process gaps and improvement suggestions, providing feedback to the Global C&O Lead for policy enhancements. Periodic Lessons Learned/Knowledge Sharing sessions will be conducted to drive continuous improvement and upskill team members.

Review Cycles

The process will be reviewed annually at a minimum by the Global C&O Powai Lead and the Head of Powai C&O. All reviews must be documented.

Change Management Protocols

Any changes to the process or its implementation must be made after consulting with all Regional Heads and communicated to all concerned parties in a timely manner.

13. Training & Development

Training Program

Team Co-Leads will ensure effective upskilling of all team members through structured training programs.
